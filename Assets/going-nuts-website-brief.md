# CLAUDE.md

## Project: GOING NUTS Website

This file is the source-of-truth project brief for Claude Code and Claude Design.

GOING NUTS is a Los Angeles creative video production company. The website should feel bold, retro, cinematic, tactile, handmade, and highly memorable, while still clearly communicating the business offer: creative direction, video production, editing, and final delivery for brands, corporates, and small to medium-sized businesses.

This is **not** The Good Bit.  
This is **not** a general marketing studio.  
This is **not** a peanut-themed novelty brand.  
This is a creative video production company with a strong visual identity.

---

# 1. Core Brand Strategy

## Core positioning

GOING NUTS is a Los Angeles creative video production company helping small and medium-sized businesses turn ideas into high-quality video content with agency-level creativity and lean production execution.

## Core line

**Agency thinking. Production-house execution.**

## Brand promise

GOING NUTS helps businesses get more creative value from every production budget.

## Key idea

**Your video is our reputation.**

Every client project also reflects on GOING NUTS as a brand, portfolio, and production partner. This is why the work is treated with care.

## What makes GOING NUTS different

GOING NUTS is not just people with cameras.

The studio brings together:

- Creative direction
- Storytelling
- Production planning
- Filming
- Editing
- Final delivery
- Practical production thinking
- Brand adaptability

## Important service principle

GOING NUTS has a bold identity, but client work should not be forced into the GOING NUTS style.

The agency adapts the tone, look, pace, and format to each client’s brand, audience, niche, and goals.

Use this line as a recurring strategic anchor:

**Our brand is loud. Your video is built around yours.**

# 4. Voice and Tone

## Brand voice

GOING NUTS sounds:

- Bold
- Direct
- Creative
- Practical
- Human
- Confident
- Slightly irreverent
- Self-aware
- Easy to understand

## Tone formula

- 50% confident production partner
- 30% creative agency brain
- 20% playful GOING NUTS energy

## The voice should not sound

Avoid sounding:

- Corporate
- Generic
- Cheap
- Desperate
- Too goofy
- Too peanut-heavy
- Too bro-y
- Too vague
- Too much like a marketing agency
- Too much like a meme brand

## Copy principles

### Lead with the idea

Start with the strongest message. Do not bury the offer under filler.

Bad:

> We provide end-to-end video production solutions for businesses seeking to improve their communication output.

Better:

> We make video content people actually want to watch.

### Keep it punchy

Use short, memorable headlines.

Examples:

- From brief to brilliant.
- Stories that stick.
- Built to be watched.
- Your video is our reputation.
- Not just people with cameras.

### Be playful, but useful

Playful language is welcome, but it should not obscure the business.

Good:

> Let’s make something nuts.

Too much:

> We crack open your brand story and serve it fresh from the shell.

Avoid overusing nut/peanut puns.

## Colour usage rules

### Backgrounds

Use warm paper as the default website background. Never use pure white as the base.

### Headlines

Use mostly black. One high-impact word can become red.

### Primary CTA

Use bright mustard with black text and an ink-like border or shadow.

### Dark panels

Use soft black with cream text. Accent icons can be mustard or teal.

### Red

Use red for high-energy CTA strips, category labels, impact words, and emphasis blocks. Do not overuse it.

### Teal

Use teal as a cool counterweight: doodles, icons, stamps, category tags, or secondary panels.

### Body copy

Default to ink on paper or cream on black. Do not set long paragraphs in red, yellow, or teal.

**Bebas Neue**

- Hero headlines

- Section headers
- CTA banners
- Case study titles
- Pricing/package titles
- Large typographic moments

## Body text

**Work Sans**

Use for:

- Paragraphs
- Service descriptions
- FAQ answers
- Navigation
- Buttons
- Forms
- Case study copy
- Practical information

## Handwritten accents

**Permanent Marker**

Use for:

- Stickers
- Stamps
- Doodles
- Tape notes
- Short handwritten-style labels
- Small accent phrases

Use sparingly.

## Mono/details

**IBM Plex Mono**

Use for:

- Tiny labels
- Production notes
- Form metadata
- Timecodes
- Category tags
- Case study specs
- Small technical details

## Typography system

- Bebas Neue = the punch
- Work Sans = the clarity
- Permanent Marker = the human scribble
- IBM Plex Mono = the production note

## Type rules

- Use condensed display type for impact.
- Keep body copy clean and readable.
- Use all-caps for headings, labels, buttons, and short notes only.
- Do not distress small text.
- Distress should come from texture overlays and masks, not unreadable fonts.

# 11. Global Website Structure Rules

## Desktop rules

Desktop can use:

- Big split hero layouts
- Large collage clusters
- Wide grids
- Horizontal process timelines
- Layered props
- Large CTA banners
- Multi-column footers
- More visual texture and atmospheric details

## Mobile rules

Mobile should use:

- Single-column sections
- Headline first
- Visual second
- Cards stacked vertically
- Shorter copy blocks
- Accordion sections
- Large tap-friendly buttons
- Simple mobile menu
- Fewer props and doodles

## Important mobile rule

Reduce decoration by around 40–50% on mobile.

Keep:

- Cream paper background
- Big Bebas Neue headlines
- Mustard CTA buttons
- Red impact words
- Teal doodles
- Paper texture
- A few tape/sticker details

Simplify:

- Tiny props
- Too many arrows
- Too many overlapping stickers
- Dense collage clusters
- Decorative peanut moments
- Tiny handwritten notes

Mobile should feel like the same bold retro cinematic website, edited down for a smaller screen.

- Full-screen or drawer navigation

# 16. Accessibility and Usability

## Contrast rules

Good combinations:

- Ink on paper
- Cream on black
- Black on mustard
- Cream on red for large text
- Cream on dark teal

Avoid:

- Yellow on cream for important text
- Red or teal for long body text
- Distressed text for small copy
- Tiny handwritten notes for important information
- Low-contrast decorative type

## Usability rules

- Buttons must look clickable.
- Form labels must be clear.
- Navigation must stay simple.
- Mobile must be easy to scan.
- Do not overload mobile with tiny collage elements.
- Keep body copy readable.
- Keep CTAs visible and direct.
